Online Help:

The Community Addressing application allows the general public to contribute missing site address locations and provide contact information for current addresses. It is used to enlist citizens and members of the general public in the continuous improvement of address and contact information across a community. The contact information (ex. name, phone, SMS, email) can then be used to supplement Emergency Alert, Reverse 911 and other Community Notification programs.

Using the Application

The Community Addressing application allows users to submit a missing site address or contact information for an existing site address. When you click on an existing address, you will see information about the current address. If you then click on the Contacts tab, the application will allow users to submit contact information for a give address.

Finding an Area of Interest

There are three primary ways to identify an area of interest. You can interact directly with the map and click on an address when you've located it. You can also use your current location or an address to find an area of interest.

Map Based Navigation

There are several ways to interact with the map:

1. Hold down the shift key and drag a box on the map to zoom in
2. Hold Down Ctrl+Shift to zoom out
3. Roll your mouse roller to zoom in/out
4. Drag the zoom slider to zoom in/out

Searching

The search box can be used to find an address:

1. Enter the address you're interested in
2. Click on the item in the list to locate the address

You can enter a partial address and then identify the correct value in the list.

Submitting Missing Addresses

Click on the location of the missing address. Enter in the appropriate address information. Click Next, and then enter your contact information. A name, email or phone is required with each new address. When you're done entering your address, click Submit.

More Information

The Community Addressing application can be used by planning and zoning, building, utility, public safety or other local government organizations to enlist members of the community in the continuous improvement of address information. It also allows citizens to enter contact information for a given address.
